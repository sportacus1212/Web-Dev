History of Boxing

Boxing is one of the oldest known sports, with its origins tracing back to ancient civilizations. The earliest depictions of boxing can be found in Sumerian carvings from as early as 3000 BCE, and the sport was formalized in Ancient Greece as part of the Olympic Games in 688 BCE.

The modern version of boxing began to take shape in the 18th century, with the establishment of rules like those in the London Prize Ring Rules and later the Queensberry Rules in 1867. These changes introduced gloves, timed rounds, and weight classes, transforming boxing into the sport we know today.

Over the centuries, boxing has grown into a global phenomenon, producing legendary athletes like Muhammad Ali, Mike Tyson, and Manny Pacquiao, who have inspired millions around the world. It remains a thrilling blend of skill, strategy, and endurance.
