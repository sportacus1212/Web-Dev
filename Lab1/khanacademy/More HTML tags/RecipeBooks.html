Bes Barmak

  • Time: 3 hours
  • Serves: High quality
Ingredients Quantity
Horse meat, flour, potatoes 2kg, 500g, 6 quantity

Step 1: Prepare the dough: Mix flour, water, and a pinch of salt to form a soft dough. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
Step 2: Roll and cut the dough: Roll out the dough thinly and cut it into diamond-shaped pieces.
Step 3: Cook the meat: Boil lamb or beef with onions, salt, and pepper until tender. Save the broth for later.
Step 4: Cook the noodles: Boil the prepared dough pieces in the meat broth until soft.
Step 5: Assemble and serve: Lay the noodles on a plate, add the meat on top, and pour the broth over. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

Baursak

  • Time: 1 hours
  • Serves: High quality
Ingredients Quantity
Oil, flour 50g, 1kg

Step 1: Prepare the dough: Mix flour, warm milk, sugar, salt, yeast, and eggs to create a soft dough. Let it rise for 1-2 hours.
Step 2: Shape the dough: Roll out the dough to a medium thickness and cut it into small squares or circles.
Step 3: Fry the baursak: Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the dough pieces until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and serve warm with jam or honey.

Nauryz Kozhe

  • Time: 2 hours
  • Serves: High quality
Ingredients Quantity
Airan, meat, bidai 800g, 3kg, 1kg

Step 1: Prepare the grains and meat: Rinse and soak grains such as barley, wheat, or rice for several hours. Cook meat (lamb, beef, or horse meat) until tender, then cut into small pieces.
Step 2: Combine ingredients: In a large pot, mix the cooked meat, soaked grains, and additional ingredients like millet, corn, and legumes. Add water or broth to cover the mixture.
Step 3: Cook the Nauryz kozhe: Bring the mixture to a boil, then simmer until the grains are fully cooked and the flavors meld together. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot.
